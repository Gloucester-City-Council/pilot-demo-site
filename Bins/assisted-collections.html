We may be able to provide a free assisted collection service if you need help to take your waste and recycling containers to the pavement because of mobility difficulties or an impairment and there is no one else in your household.

If you have received a review letter, we are aware that some residents didn't receive the form.

If you would like to continue with your assisted collection please email your collection address to environment@gloucester.gov.uk

You do not need to complete the form on this page.

Who is eligible

‚úì You may be eligible if:

  • You have mobility difficulties or an impairment that prevents you from taking bins to the pavement
  • There is no one else in your household who can help
  • You live in a property with individual bins (not communal bin stores)

‚úï This service is not available to:

  • Residents that use communal bin stores
  • The original person who applied for an assisted collection and is no longer living at the property
  • Residents that have restricted access for example caravan parks, narrow or private roads, steps or steep driveways, length of driveway or locked gates

Important: If you are receiving assisted collections but no longer need the service, you must inform us as soon as possible.

Important information

üìã Proof of eligibility

We may ask for proof of eligibility and we reserve the right to suspend or withdraw this service at any time although we would notify you if the service was to be withdrawn.

üö™ Access requirements

You will need to ensure the collection crew has access to your containers from 7am on the day of collection. If they are unable to gain access, then there will be no further collection until your next scheduled collection day.

üìÖ Collection schedule

Your collection days will remain the same as your regular schedule. The crew will collect your bins from an agreed location and return them after emptying.
