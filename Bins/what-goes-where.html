What goes in which bin

Don't just recycle ‚Äî recycle right. Getting it right helps protect the environment and saves money. Use this guide to know exactly what goes in each bin.

Green box (plastics and metals)

Collected every week.

You can put in:

  • Plastic bottles (drinks, shampoo, cleaning products)
  • Plastic pots (yoghurt pots, margarine tubs)
  • Plastic tubs (ice cream, butter)
  • Plastic trays (meat, fruit, vegetables)
  • Metal cans and tins (food and drink cans)
  • Aerosols (empty only)
  • Aluminium foil (clean and scrunched into a ball)
  • Metal lids and bottle tops

Do not put in:

  • Plastic bags or carrier bags
  • Polystyrene or styrofoam
  • Plastic film or cling film
  • Black plastic trays
  • Crisp packets or sweet wrappers

Top tips: Rinse items and squash bottles to save space. Remove lids from plastic bottles ‚Äî metal lids can go in, plastic lids go in general waste.

Green box (glass)

Collected every week.

You can put in:

  • Glass bottles (wine, beer, spirits)
  • Glass jars (jam, sauces, baby food)

Do not put in:

  • Drinking glasses or tumblers
  • Pyrex or ovenware
  • Window glass or mirrors
  • Light bulbs
  • Crystal

Top tips: Rinse bottles and jars. You can leave labels on. Remove lids ‚Äî metal lids go in green box 1, plastic lids go in general waste.

Blue bag (paper and cardboard)

Collected every week.

You can put in:

  • Cardboard boxes (flatten them first)
  • Paper and writing paper
  • Newspapers and magazines
  • Envelopes (including with windows)
  • Junk mail and leaflets
  • Greetings cards (remove batteries and glitter)
  • Cereal and food boxes
  • Cardboard egg boxes

Do not put in:

  • Food-contaminated cardboard (like pizza boxes with grease)
  • Shredded paper
  • Wallpaper
  • Tissues or paper towels
  • Wrapping paper with glitter or foil

Top tips: Flatten cardboard boxes to save space. Remove plastic windows from envelopes (these go in general waste). If cardboard is wet or has food on it, put it in your black bin.

Food caddy (food waste)

Collected every week.

You can put in:

  • All cooked food
  • All raw food
  • Plate scrapings
  • Fruit and vegetable peelings
  • Meat, fish and bones
  • Dairy products (cheese, eggs)
  • Bread, cakes and pastries
  • Tea bags and coffee grounds
  • Out of date food (remove packaging)

Do not put in:

  • Any packaging or wrapping
  • Liquids or oil
  • Pet waste

Top tips: Line your caddy with newspaper or use compostable bags (available free from the council). You can put food waste straight in without a liner if you prefer. Empty your caddy every week to prevent smells.

Black bin (general waste)

Collected every two weeks.

This is for everything that cannot be recycled. Because it's collected less often than recycling, you should recycle as much as possible to avoid your bin filling up.

Common items for general waste:

  • Nappies and sanitary products
  • Plastic bags and carrier bags
  • Polystyrene packaging
  • Cling film and plastic film
  • Tissues and kitchen roll
  • Crisp packets and sweet wrappers
  • Pet waste (bagged)
  • Broken glass
  • Clothes and textiles (or donate them)

Top tips: If your bin is full, do not leave extra bags beside it. Consider if you're recycling everything you can. You can request an extra bin if you have a larger household.

Brown bin (garden waste) - subscription service

Collected every two weeks from February to November. Winter break in December and January. ¬£60 per year subscription required.

You can put in:

  • Grass cuttings and lawn clippings
  • Leaves
  • Small branches and twigs
  • Hedge trimmings
  • Plants and flowers (including weeds)
  • Windfall fruit

Do not put in:

  • Soil or turf
  • Tree stumps or large branches
  • Japanese knotweed
  • Diseased plants
  • Pet waste
  • Food waste (use your food caddy)

Items that need special disposal

These items cannot go in any of your bins. Take them to a recycling centre or arrange special collection:

  • Electrical items ‚Äî small electricals can be taken to recycling centres. Find out how to dispose of batteries and electricals
  • Textiles and clothes ‚Äî donate to charity shops or textile banks. Find textile recycling points
  • Bulky waste ‚Äî furniture, white goods, mattresses. Book a collection
  • Hazardous waste ‚Äî paint, chemicals, asbestos. Dispose of hazardous waste safely
  • Batteries ‚Äî take to supermarkets or recycling centres. Battery recycling locations

Why getting it right matters

When you recycle correctly:

  • Materials get turned into new products instead of going to landfill
  • We save money that can be spent on other council services
  • We reduce carbon emissions and protect the environment
  • We create local jobs in the recycling industry

When recycling is contaminated (wrong items mixed in), the entire load can be rejected and sent to landfill. This costs money and wastes everyone's effort.

Quick reminders

  • Rinse food containers before recycling
  • Squash bottles and flatten cardboard
  • Empty your food caddy every week
  • Remove lids from bottles and jars
