What we collect

‚úì You can put in your brown bin

  • Grass cuttings
  • Lawn clippings
  • Leaves
  • Small branches (under 10cm)
  • Twigs
  • Hedge trimmings
  • Plants & flowers
  • Weeds
  • Windfall fruit
  • Dead flower heads

‚úï Do not put in your brown bin

  • Soil or turf
  • Tree stumps or large branches
  • Japanese knotweed (invasive species)
  • Diseased plants (put in black bin to prevent spread)
  • Pet waste
  • Food waste (use your food caddy instead)
  • Plant pots, stones or gravel

Common questions

Can I have more than one brown bin?

Yes. You pay ¬£60 for each additional bin. Subscribe for your first bin, then order extra bins separately.

What if I only want it for part of the year?

The subscription is for a full 12 months. You can cancel and get a refund if you've had fewer than 3 collections.

Do I get a discount if I'm on Council Tax Support?

Not currently, but we're reviewing this. The full price is ¬£60 for everyone.

What if I move house?

If you move within Gloucester, your subscription moves with you. Contact us with your new address. If you move outside Gloucester, you can get a refund for unused months.

What happens to my garden waste?

It's taken to a composting facility where it's turned into soil conditioner used by farmers and gardeners.

What if my bin isn't collected?

Wait until the next working day (crews sometimes run late), then report a missed collection. We'll return within 2 working days.

Can I put garden waste in my black bin instead?

You can, but your black bin is only collected every two weeks and may fill up quickly. Garden waste can also be composted at home for free.
